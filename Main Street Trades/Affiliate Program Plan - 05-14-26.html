Plan · 05-14-26

Affiliate program plan.

How influencers send signups to Main Street Trades, how the system tracks who paid through whose link, and how they get paid. Built on what we already have, not on what we'd have to build.

Bottom line

Use Whop's built-in affiliate program for the tracking, attribution, and payout — it ships with everything we need. Layer a pretty-URL redirect on top (mainstreettrades.com/r/<name>) so each influencer gets a brandable link. Zero custom infrastructure. Live in one afternoon.

01 / The decisionWhy Whop's native affiliate, not a custom build

Whop is already the payment processor, the member gate, and the Discord access layer. Their affiliate program is wired directly into checkout — which means attribution is bulletproof (it's not heuristic UTM matching, it's the actual purchase record). The alternatives all involve either rebuilding what Whop already does, or duct-taping a third party into a stack it wasn't designed for.

02 / Commission structureWhat we pay influencers, and why

Whop lets us set a default rate for "anyone with a link" and then override it for specific influencers we recruit. The ladder below is calibrated for the trading-creator economy — high enough to compete with Geneva, Discord/Skool referrals, and other stock-alert rooms, low enough to keep unit economics intact at $67/mo.

Default · global
30%
Anyone who signs up as an affiliate. Recurring while the referred member stays subscribed. Same as Whop's default — we're not the cheapest, not the most generous.
Creator · invited
40%
Influencers we proactively recruit. YouTube/IG/TikTok in finance, 10K–250K followers. Recurring. The extra 10pts is what makes them choose us over a competitor's 30%.
Partner · top
50%
First-month boost + 25% recurring for our top 3 partners (250K+ or proven ROI). Custom-negotiated. Whop supports per-user rates and split first-payment-vs-recurring rules.
Unit economics check: Premium is $67/mo. At 40% recurring, we pay $26.80/mo per referred member. Cost-to-acquire on paid ads usually runs $50–80 per Discord signup with a 20–40% retention curve. Affiliate is cheaper AND retains better because the trust came from a creator, not an ad.

04 / Pretty URL layerBranded links that survive attribution

Whop's affiliate URLs work but look like spam (long, query-string-heavy). Influencers convert better when the link is short and on-brand. Solution: a 302 redirect from mainstreettrades.com/r/<slug> to their full Whop URL. Vercel handles this declaratively in vercel.json — no app server, no database, no clicks-tracking middleware needed (Whop is already counting clicks on its end).

Add a redirects block to vercel.json:

// vercel.json — add alongside existing keys
"redirects": [
  {
    "source":      "/r/charlie",
    "destination": "https://whop.com/main-street-trades/?a=CHARLIE_CODE",
    "permanent":   false
  },
  {
    "source":      "/r/:slug",
    "destination": "https://whop.com/main-street-trades/?a=:slug",
    "permanent":   false
  }
]

The first rule is an explicit override (use it when Whop generates a code that doesn't match the influencer's name). The second is a catch-all fallback for influencers whose Whop affiliate code matches their preferred slug. Browser hits Whop with the affiliate param intact → Whop credits the affiliate → done.

Verify before going live: Whop's actual query parameter name (?a= vs ?ref= vs ?aff=) isn't fixed in their docs. Step 4 of the setup checklist resolves this — adjust the destination string in vercel.json once we've copied a real affiliate link.

Adding a new influencer becomes a 30-second edit: append one entry to the redirects array, commit, Vercel redeploys in ~20s. No code review needed for the marketing team to do this themselves once the pattern is set.

05 / Influencer recruitmentWhere they come from and what they get

Sourcing

  • YouTube — search "stock alerts review", "options trading discord review", "I tried [competitor]". Channels with 10K–250K subs that review trading rooms are the bullseye.
  • X / Twitter Finance — accounts that post P&L screenshots, day-trade recaps, options flow. 5K–100K followers. Engagement matters more than reach.
  • TikTok & Reels — "fintok" and stock-alert reaction creators. Smaller followings but high conversion on a 10-second hook.
  • Existing competitor affiliates — anyone publicly promoting a competing alert room. They already understand the business model.

  • A one-page brand sheet: brand colors, logo, the "Main Street, not Wall Street" positioning line, and 3 tweet-ready taglines.
  • Required disclosure language (see section 06).

06 / ComplianceFTC + finance niche caveats

Trading content is the highest-scrutiny niche in affiliate marketing. The FTC has gone after stock-tip rooms for undisclosed paid promotion. We require — in writing, as part of the affiliate invite — that every promoting creator:

  • Discloses they receive a commission. Bare minimum: "Affiliate link — I earn a commission when you join." Better: that exact line in the video caption AND spoken in the video.
  • Includes the standard "Not financial advice. Trading involves risk of loss." disclaimer.
  • Does not promise specific returns or use phrasing like "guaranteed profits" or "[N]x your money."
  • Does not impersonate Main Street Trades, Dan, or any named member.

Whop's affiliate TOS already covers most of this, but we send it again on our invite so there's a paper trail if someone goes rogue. Violation = affiliate access revoked, unpaid commissions forfeited, per the Whop TOS.

07 / Operational rhythmHow this runs once it's live

  • Daily: nothing. Whop handles attribution and tracking automatically.
  • Weekly: 15-minute glance at the affiliate dashboard. Top 3 affiliates, conversion rate, anyone whose performance dropped.
  • Monthly: outreach to 5–10 new prospective creators. DM the top performer with a personal thank-you and ask for content collaboration. Bump anyone over $1K in monthly referred revenue to the Partner tier.
  • Payouts: handled by Whop. 30-day clawback hold (refund protection) then auto-paid to the affiliate. We never touch the money. 1099 paperwork for US affiliates over $600/yr is Whop's responsibility, not ours.
  • Quarterly: review commission ladder. If we're at 30 active affiliates and revenue scales, consider raising the default rate to 35% to recruit faster, or running a 60-day promo at 50% to spike sign-ups.
